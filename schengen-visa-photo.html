Schengen Visa Photo FAQ | EU Visa Photo Questions

Common questions about Schengen visa photos and EU visa requirements

What size should Schengen visa photos be?

Schengen visa photos must be exactly 35mm wide by 45mm tall (3.5cm × 4.5cm). This is different from standard passport photos which are typically 50×50mm. Our tool automatically generates photos in the correct 35×45mm format required for EU visa applications.

Can I use this photo for all EU countries?

Yes! Photos generated by our tool meet the requirements for all 27 Schengen Agreement countries including Germany, France, Spain, Italy, Netherlands, and all other EU nations that issue Schengen visas.

How do I ensure my face is positioned correctly?

Our automatic face detection positions your head within the correct guidelines. Your head should occupy 70-80% of the photo height (32-36mm), with eyes positioned 50-69% from the bottom of the photo.

What background color is required for Schengen visas?

All Schengen visa photos require a plain white background with no shadows or patterns. Our tool automatically removes your existing background and replaces it with the required white background.

Are these photos accepted by consulates?

Yes, photos generated by our tool meet all official Schengen visa photo requirements and are accepted by EU consulates worldwide. We follow the exact specifications outlined in the Common Consular Instructions.

Can I print these photos at home?

Absolutely! Our tool generates high-resolution 600 DPI photos perfect for home printing. We also provide a print-ready PDF with multiple photos arranged for efficient printing on standard photo paper.

What if I wear glasses or hijab?

Glasses are generally acceptable if there's no glare and your eyes are clearly visible. Religious headwear like hijab is permitted if it doesn't obscure your face. Our tool accommodates both while maintaining compliance.

How recent should my photo be?

Schengen visa photos should be taken within the last 6 months to accurately represent your current appearance. The photo must show your natural skin tone and current hairstyle.

Schengen Visa Photo Tips | EU Visa Application Success

Professional tips for perfect Schengen visa photos and successful EU visa applications

Photo Quality

Use good lighting and take the photo straight-on. Avoid shadows on your face or background. Natural daylight provides the best results for visa photos.

Dress Code

Wear everyday clothes in dark colors that contrast with the white background. Avoid white or very light colored clothing that might blend with the background.

Facial Expression

Maintain a neutral expression with your mouth closed. Look directly at the camera with both eyes open and visible. Avoid smiling or frowning.

Printing Tips

Print on matte photo paper for best results. Ensure your printer is set to highest quality and actual size (100%) without scaling to fit the page.

Schengen Visa Application Resources | EU Visa Help

Helpful resources for your Schengen visa application process

  • Completed visa application form
  • Valid passport with 2 blank pages
  • 2 recent passport photos (35×45mm)
  • Travel insurance (minimum €30,000)
  • Flight itinerary and accommodation proof
  • Financial proof (bank statements)

Application Timeline

  • Apply 15 days to 6 months before travel
  • Processing time: 15 calendar days

  • Collect passport after approval
