Visa & Work Permit Types (2026)

EU/EEA/Swiss citizens need no permit - just register at the gemeente. For everyone else, these are the main routes. Salary thresholds are gross monthly, EXCLUDING the 8% holiday allowance, and are indexed every January.

RouteRequirements (2026)Key Facts
Highly Skilled Migrant
(Kennismigrant / HSM)		 Job offer from an IND recognized sponsor paying at least 5,942 EUR/month (age 30+) or 4,357 EUR/month (under 30). No degree requirement - salary is the only test. The standard expat route: employer applies, decision typically in 2-4 weeks. No labour market test. Salary must be paid monthly to your bank account and be market-conform.
EU Blue Card Higher-education degree (or 3+ years senior IT experience) + salary of 5,942 EUR/month, or 4,754 EUR/month for graduates within 3 years of their degree. Slightly slower than HSM but portable: easier moves to other EU countries and family rights. Employer does not need to be a recognized sponsor.
Orientation Year
(Zoekjaar)		 Graduated within the last 3 years from a Dutch university OR a top-200 world-ranked university, or completed PhD/research in NL. 1-year permit to live and work freely (no sponsor needed) while job hunting. Afterwards you qualify for the reduced HSM threshold of only 3,122 EUR/month - a massive discount.
ICT Permit Intra-corporate transfer from a branch outside the EU; manager, specialist, or trainee. Same age-based salary thresholds as HSM. Max 3 years (1 year trainees); tied to the corporate group.
Self-Employed / DAFT Points-based test on business plan and added value. US citizens: the Dutch-American Friendship Treaty (DAFT) route needs only ~4,500 EUR invested in a Dutch business. DAFT is one of the easiest self-employment visas in Europe for Americans; 2-year permit, renewable.
Startup Visa Innovative business idea + agreement with an approved facilitator (incubator/mentor). 1 year to build the company, then switch to the self-employed permit.

The 30% Ruling (Expat Tax Break)

Recruited from abroad (150+ km from the border)? You can receive 30% of your gross salary tax-free for up to 5 years. 2026 requirements: taxable salary of at least 48,013 EUR/year (36,497 EUR if under 30 with a master's degree). The benefit is capped at the Balkenende norm (~262,000 EUR salary). Important update: the previously announced 30/20/10 phase-down was REVERSED - the rate stays a flat 30% through 2026, then drops to a flat 27% from January 2027 (existing pre-2024 users keep 30% under transitional rules). Apply within 4 months of starting - it is worth roughly 500-1,500 EUR/month net for typical expat salaries.

Bringing Your Family

QuestionAnswer (2026 rules)
Can my spouse and children come?Yes, immediately - family applications are filed together with yours and decided at the same time. No waiting period, no separate income requirement beyond your salary threshold.
Can my spouse work?Yes - the partner of an HSM or Blue Card holder gets a permit stating "arbeid vrij toegestaan" (work freely allowed). Any job, any employer, no sponsor needed. This makes NL one of the best countries for dual-career expat couples.
Language requirements?None before arrival for HSM/Blue Card families. Civic integration (inburgering) only applies later to some categories - HSM holders and their partners are exempt while on the HSM permit.
Child benefits?Once registered, you get kinderbijslag (295-422 EUR per quarter per child depending on age, 2026) from the SVB, plus income-dependent kindgebonden budget and kinderopvangtoeslag (childcare allowance).

Your Rights as a Non-EU Worker

QuestionAnswer Under Dutch Law
Can I switch employers? Yes. The HSM permit is not tied to one employer - the new employer just needs to be a recognized sponsor and pay the threshold salary. The new sponsor notifies the IND; you do not file a new application. Check the IND public register of recognized sponsors before signing.
What if I lose my job? You get a 3-month search period from the end of your contract to find a new qualifying employer, keeping your residence rights. If you worked 26+ weeks, you may also be entitled to WW unemployment benefit (~70-75% of salary) like any Dutch worker.
Can my employer lock me in for X years? No. You can always resign with the contractual notice period (typically 1 month). Clauses forbidding resignation because the employer "sponsored your visa" are unenforceable. Study-cost repayment clauses are banned for mandatory training since August 2022.
Non-compete clauses? Only valid in writing with an adult employee. In temporary contracts they are void unless the employer gives specific written justification. Dutch courts frequently strike down or limit unreasonable non-competes; challenge via a lawyer or your union.
How do I get permanent residence? After 5 years of continuous legal residence: Dutch permanent residence or EU long-term residence. Requires passing the inburgering (civic integration) exam - language level A2 today, with B1 planned. Time on HSM/Blue Card counts fully.
And citizenship? Currently 5 years + inburgering + renouncing your original nationality (the Netherlands generally does NOT allow dual citizenship on naturalization, with exceptions e.g. spouses of Dutch citizens). Heads up: the current government has proposed extending the requirement to 10 years and B1 Dutch - a bill, not yet law in mid-2026. If you will hit 5 years soon, apply promptly.

Red Flags in Job Offers

  • Employer not in the IND recognized-sponsor register - verify on ind.nl before signing an "HSM" offer.
  • "Salary" partly paid as unguaranteed bonus or expenses - the IND counts only fixed, guaranteed, bank-paid salary; you risk permit refusal.
  • Being charged for your own sponsorship or permit fees - sponsor obligations are the employer's; recruiters charging workers is a labour-exploitation signal.
  • Forced fake self-employment (schijnzelfstandigheid) - "join as a zzp'er" to dodge the threshold; enforcement tightened sharply since 2025.
  • "You cannot resign for 2 years" - unenforceable; you can always resign with notice.
  • Passport confiscation - illegal; report to police (0900-8844).

Family money tips (Amsterdam): (1) Apply for kinderbijslag at the SVB right after BRP registration. (2) Kinderopvangtoeslag requires BOTH parents working (or studying) - apply via Belastingdienst within 3 months of starting daycare. The Three Pension Pillars

PillarWhat It IsShould You Care?
1. AOW (state pension)Flat state pension from age 67. You accrue 2% for every year you live/work in NL between 17 and 67.Automatic. Arriving at 35 means ~64% of full AOW - a gap you should fill with pillar 2/3 savings.
2. Employer pensionSector or company funds (ABP, PFZW, etc.); both employer and employee contribute. Being reformed to defined-contribution under the Wet toekomst pensioenen (transition through 2028).Check your contract - not legally mandatory in every sector. Tech startups sometimes offer none; factor that into salary talks.
3. Private (lijfrente / banksparen)Tax-deductible annuity saving up to your jaarruimte (unused pension room), via banks or brokers (e.g. Brand New Day, DEGIRO pension).Great if pillar 2 is weak - contributions reduce Box 1 income now and escape Box 3 entirely.

Money Survival Guide

TopicWhat You Need to Know
Cards, not cashThe Netherlands is aggressively cashless - many supermarkets, cafes, and even market stalls are PIN-only ("PIN alleen"). The catch: some places take only Maestro/debit, and foreign CREDIT cards often fail. Get a Dutch debit card fast.
iDEALThe national online payment standard - bank-to-bank payment from your own banking app. Needed for webshops, tax payments, ticket sites. Works automatically once you have a Dutch account.
Tikkie & payment requestsSplitting costs is a national sport. Install Tikkie (works with any Dutch IBAN) - refusing to pay a Tikkie is a social crime.
Credit cards & credit scoresCredit cards are rare and mostly deferred-debit. There is no credit-score culture; instead the BKR register logs debts - a BKR listing can block mortgages, so avoid consumer credit and buy-now-pay-later.
Automatic paymentsRent, energy, insurance run on SEPA direct debit (automatische incasso). You can reverse an unauthorized incasso within 8 weeks with one tap.
Sending money homeUse Wise/Revolut - Dutch bank international fees outside SEPA are mediocre.
Recommended newcomer setup: Week 1: open bunq with your passport so salary and iDEAL work immediately. After BSN: open ING or ABN AMRO as your long-term main account, keep Wise/Revolut for transfers home. Install Tikkie day one.

Childcare (Kinderopvang) - How It Works

StepDetails
1. The cost structureDaycare (kinderdagverblijf, age 0-4) costs ~10-11.50 EUR/hour full price - easily 1,600-2,000 EUR/month for 3 days/week. Sounds terrifying; keep reading.
2. KinderopvangtoeslagThe childcare allowance refunds 33-96% of costs up to a max hourly rate (~10.90 EUR for daycare in 2026), depending on household income. BOTH parents must work or study. Apply via Belastingdienst with DigiD within 3 months.
3. Net resultA middle-income family typically pays 300-700 EUR/month net for 3 days of daycare. The long-promised "nearly free childcare" reform has been postponed to ~2029.
4. WaitlistsAmsterdam daycare waitlists run 6-12 months - register during pregnancy. Alternatives: gastouder (childminder, also toeslag-eligible, often cheaper), peuterspeelzaal part-time from age 2.
5. School age (4+)School is free; BSO (after-school care until ~18:30) is toeslag-eligible like daycare.
